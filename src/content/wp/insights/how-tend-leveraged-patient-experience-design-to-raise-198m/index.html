How Tend Leveraged Patient Experience Design to Raise $198M

All healthcare businesses can learn from this analysis of Tend’s approach to patient experience design, which has contributed to the brand’s rapid expansion and success.

Tend positions itself as the first dentist that people actually *want* to visit. Love them or loathe them, healthcare business owners can all learn from Tend’s innovative approach to achieving massive growth through a modern blend of branding, design, and technology.

From its founding in 2019, Tend has brought in $200 million worth of funding and opened 28 offices nationwide. They’ve seen this explosive growth because of their singular focus on improving the patient experience.

Our Take on Tend’s Winning Patient Experience Strategy

Let’s break down this statement into some actionable tactics that healthcare business owners can use to grow like Tend. As Doug laid out in his medium post mentioned above, patient experience is about so much more than the feel a patient gets when they walk in the door of your business. 

While arriving for a first appointment is obviously an important moment and one that Tend has spent a lot of effort optimizing, they’ve correctly identified that in today’s digital age, first impressions happen long before that. The patient journey is a long and meandering path, and as research about the patient journey from Google shows us, most people take 2+ weeks to find a provider, performing many searches along the way and interacting with a brand on several different mediums, like a website, social media and reviews sites, before ultimately booking an appointment. Inevitably, most patients will also perform the same deep dive on the digital presence of a competitor as well. An enormous part of Tend’s winning strategy is how good they’ve been in providing a consistent patient experience across all of those digital touchpoints before solidifying those positive experiences when that patient finally does arrive in one of their studios. 

Here’s where they’ve actioned effectively: 

  1. Tend is visible when patients want to find a dentist, thanks to a strong digital marketing presence that combines search engine optimization, social media and advertising to great success.
  2. Tend grows through a focus on improving “phygital” experiences for patients – physical, in-person care and the digital experiences which lead them there. They’ve done the legwork of putting themselves in the patient’s shoes and actively worked towards improving the places where patients come in contact with their business, online and in-person.
  3. Tend cements these positive experiences in patients’ minds through excellent and consistent branding– their website, socials, ads and studios all uniformly communicate the feeling they want to convey. Their attention to brand conveys polished professionalism before a patient even walks through the door or speaks to a member of their team.

How to Apply Tend’s Success to Your Practice

You don’t need a $198M war chest to implement some of Tend’s most effective strategies. Whether you’re a venture-backed DSO or a single practitioner looking to grow, emulating the best of these tactics shouldn’t be out of reach. Here’s how to start:

Focus on visibility

Focus on your business’ visibility by working to ensure you’re present when patients are searching for a dentist. Brushing up on search engine optimization tactics or working with an agency who specializes in online visibility is a good first step.

Enhance the touchpoints along the patient journey

Identify the places where patients will come in contact with your business and ask yourself if each one serves their needs as well as it could. These touchpoints could be physical, like your office, or digital, like your website, paperwork, social posts, emails and ads. Consider partnering with someone who can help with touchpoints that are outside of your office.

Strengthen your brand

Establish or maintain your business’ brand. Visual branding is powerful and communicates a lot about your professionalism, enabling or detracting from your ability to attract quality patients, team members and investment. Consider working with a brand designer or agency who can help.
